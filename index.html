About Me

I am an undergraduate student researcher within the Institute for Experiential Robotics at Northeastern University, Boston MA, USA. I am pursuing the combined B.S. degree of computer engineering and computer science. My research interests include sustainability, swarm robotics, computer vision, path planning, and automation.

My professional interest is robotics research. I would like to explore how mobile robotics can battle the effects of the climate change. Currently I am working on a heterogenous system called T.R.A.S.H which will identify and pick up litter in hard-to-access places. Also, I am part of Northeastern’s RASCAL team, researching an innovative MARs ISRU system in response to a nationwide challenge from NASA.

My personal interests include being a member of the Jewish Student Union at Northeastern University and notetaking for Northeastern’s Disability Resource Center. In my spare time, I like to travel, play basketball, camp, and cook.
